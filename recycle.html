Recycling Guide

Proper recycling helps conserve resources, reduce landfill waste, and protect the environment. Use this guide to learn how to recycle common materials correctly.

Recycling Basics

Before recycling, remember to:

  • Rinse containers to remove food residue
  • Remove lids and caps (check local guidelines)
  • Flatten cardboard boxes to save space
  • Check local recycling rules - they vary by location
  • When in doubt, throw it out (contamination can ruin entire batches)

Recycling Guide Table

Material Recyclable? Method Preparation Required
Plastic Bottles (1 & 2) Yes Curbside Recycling Rinse, remove caps
Glass Bottles & Jars Yes Curbside or Drop-off Rinse, remove lids
Aluminum Cans Yes Curbside Recycling Rinse, can be crushed
Cardboard Yes Curbside Recycling Flatten, remove tape
Paper (Office, Newspaper) Yes Curbside Recycling Keep dry, remove plastic windows
Plastic Bags Sometimes Store Drop-off Clean, dry, bundled together
Batteries Special Recycling Designated Drop-off Never put in regular recycling
Electronics E-Waste Programs Special Collection Remove batteries if possible

Common Recycling Mistakes to Avoid

Pizza Boxes

Only recycle if clean. Grease-soaked parts should go in compost or trash.

Broken Glass

Wrap in paper and dispose in trash - it's dangerous for recycling workers.

Plastic Utensils

Usually not recyclable. Use reusable utensils instead.

The 3 R's Hierarchy

1. REDUCE

Most effective. Use less stuff in the first place.

2. REUSE

Find new uses for items instead of throwing them away.

3. RECYCLE

Last option. Process materials into new products.
